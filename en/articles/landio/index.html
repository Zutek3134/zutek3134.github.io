Land-io Analysis　

2023/06/23 ~ 5 min 數學 其他

Land-io is a free Discord activity. According to their blog post1:

Claim territory and become outstanding in your field(s).

And according to their FAQ2:

In Land-io, players claim territory by moving around, making trails, and enclosing plots of land to gain them as their own.

The goal is to have the most land claimed when game time runs out, but watch out for other players! If someone runs into your trail, you'll lose all the land you've collected."

The player with the most land at the end of the 5 minutes wins the game.

Inside the activity tab3:

Pick a character and encircle new territory. Run into trails and knock down foes - careful not to hit your own!

I. How To Play　

The tutorial shows four simple steps to play Land-io:

  1. Claim land

    “Claim” land by walking out then back in of your existing “territory.”

  2. Knockout players

    A player is “vulnerable” while outside of their own territory, leaving a trail as they walk. Overlap with the trail to “knock them out.”

  3. Don't knockout yourself

    Walking on your own trail will “knock yourself out.”

  4. Controls

    To move around the 2D plane environment, use arrow keys or WASD.

    To stop moving, use space bar.

    You cannot pause nor stop the game with esc. Pausing doesn't exist anyway.

II. Setup　

1. Before Entering Game　

Before a player joins the game, they are inside the “lobby.” There, players can choose whether to join the game or spectate.

Did you know?

When a round ends and the results shows up, the lobby is hidden. Players are forced to watch the result screen regardless of their current status (in lobby, in game, or spectating).

If a player chooses to play, they must choose an “element” as their in-game character, which are:

  1. Fungi
  2. Lava
  3. Crystal
  4. Water

Did you know?

When hovering above an unselected element, its eyes follows the horizontal axis of the mouse.

Hovering above a selected element will play its idle animation.

The differences between them are different looks and sound effects. There are no skills nor abilities. Choosing any character affects no gameplay and gives no advantages.

Each element has 4 variants, which means there could be 4 players of the same element.

Up until now, there are no evidence that other variants are chosen in any particular order. However, the first used variant is always Fungi, Lava, Crystal, or Water, depending on the user's choice.

Fungi Lava Crystal Water
Grass Flame Amethyst Ocean
Moss Sulphur Citrine Lake
Bark Ember Rose Reef

When all variants of an element is used, the button greys out and new players could no longer select it.

With some simple math, we can easily calculate the maximum number of players in the game, which is 16.

\(4\) (types of elements) \(\times 4\) (variants avaliable) \(= 16\) (possible players)

Did you know?

In the description tab inside “Discord's Birthday,” Land-io is labelled as 1 - 8 players3. However, in the activity tab, it says up to 16 participants.

2. After Entering Game　

When a player joins the game, they are granted 9 blocks and spawns in the middle of their own territory. A player can spawn anywhere in the map with at least one edge touching the border of it.

Each round lasts exactly 3 minutes.

Did you know?

In the description tab inside “Discord's Birthday,” Land-io is labelled as 5+ min3. However, a round is 3 minutes and the result screen is 22 seconds.

3. Collectibles　

Unlike most games, there are no collectibles available in the game.

III. Objectives　

In Land-io, everything is constant and nothing changes throughout the entire round except your territory. Players do not gain speed nor resistence as they progress.

The main goal, is to claim as many land as possible.

Awards　

When a round ends, the game gives out awards; any player who is still in the activity when it ends has a chance of winning one.

If a player has not left the activity (as in, not playing Land-io anymore), even if they exited the round and is spectating or idle in lobby, they can still win an award.

Did you know?

Although the award “Survivalist” has a maximum possible value of 3 minutes, which is a no-death run, it would still be displayed as 2:59 in the Winners tab time column.

Maximum Possible Knockouts


To calculate the maximum possible knockouts in a round, we need to find out the shortest amount of time to knock someone out.

Method 1
Assume the best situation where one player could be knocked out everytime one block is travelled. Calculate the speed of player and then divide by the time.

The speed of the player is the distance covered per second. I took the first screenshot and immediately pressed the down arrow key; then, as soon as I hit the bottom, I screenshotted again.

Therefore, the result is \(51 \div 10 = 5.1\) blocks per second. To know where “51” came from, check Chapter V.

And the maximum possible knockouts per round is \(3 \times 60 \div 5.1 \approx 35\)

Although it's possible, it's not probable.

Method 2
Now imagine another scenario where there are only 2 players playing. One player, A, keeps knocking the other, B, out. B just keeps respawning and continuously spawns merely 5 blocks away from A.

The most efficient way for A to knock B out is for A to claim B's territory. For this to happen, A has to claim the entire map first.

As soon as the game starts, A and B conveniently spawns at the left and right upper corner of the map, both starts claiming half of the map by walking towards each other.

A: right 26, down 51, left 26, up 51, right 26; 31 seconds.
B: left 25, down 51, right 25, up 51, left 25; 30 seconds.

Since B is faster than A, B walks into A's territory before A walks into B's, successfully knocking B out. B then constantly spawns just 5 blocks in front of A's direction.

After some play testing, turns out it takes about 5 seconds to respawn.

So after A's first knockout, B spends 5 seconds respawning, and A spends 1 second arriving to B's new territory. Enclosing B's new territory takes up \(1 \times \frac{3}{5.1} \approx 0.59\) seconds.

In total, this whole respawn-knockout process is around \(5 + 1 + 0.59 = 6.59\) seconds.

This process starts immediately after the first knockout, with the remaining time as “2 min 29 sec,” or 149 seconds.

\(149 \div 6.59 \approx 22.6\), which means this process can be repeated 22 times. Therefore, the maximum possible knockouts for 2 players is 23.

IV. Interactions　

By default, players join a public room with their personal information (such as profile picture and username) are hidden. There is a button to toggle whether to play anonymously or not.

Players cannot create rooms and are assigned to one randomly. Switching rooms is not an option; however, there is a workaround which is simply changing voice channels.

At the bottom-right corner, it is possible to switch to a private room. While a public room includes players from other servers, a private room only includes players within the same voice channel.

If a player wants to switch to a private room, the majority of the players in the same voice channels have to vote for this change. Everyone in the same voice channel must play in the same room.

By default, players in a private room are not anonymous.

There are no in-game way to communicate with anyone, you can only chat with the ones in the same server.

V. Sounds　

There is only one background music in Land-io, it plays repeatedly throughout the entire game regardless of the player's status or room.

There are, however, many sound effects; different elements have their own unique sounds.

Most sounds are the same, for example:

  • buttons;
  • countdown;
  • time's-up bell;
  • result screen animation.
Did you know?

A short “ding” plays when the remaining time is 10 seconds, and the countdown text turns red. This is the only time-is-almost-up alert in the game.

Element-wise sounds include:

  • choosing an element;
  • entering the map;
  • knocking out others (exclusing yourself);

VI. Maps　

There is only 1 default map, it shows up when the game is loading:

We can estimate the size of the map by calculating:

Solve

A player has 9 blocks upon spawning, and at the same time the score board displays “\(1\%\)” at the total claimed land column

Let the area of the map \(x\), \(\frac{9}{x} = 0.01\)

Obviously, \(x = 900\), so there are 900 blocks

We presume the map is a square

Since \(\sqrt{900} = 30\), the map is a square with length and width of 30

The most accurate way is to just count the blocks on the map, as below:

There are 51 blocks on each side, so the area of the map is \(51 \times 51 = 2601\) squared unit.

We can then further calculate the maximum area of “1%”

\(2601 \times 0.01 = 26.01\)
\(\sqrt{26} \approx 5.09\)
\((5 + 1)^2 \div 2601 \approx 0.014\)
\((5 + 1)(5 + 2) \div 2601 \approx 0.016\)
\((5 + 2)^2 \div 2601 \approx 0.019\)

In conclusion, the biggest possible area to be 1% is a 6 by 6 square.
