The Origin of Children's Day　

2023/04/03 ~ 4 min 社會

It'll be Children's Day tomrrow in Taiwan, but have you ever wondered why this day exists? Why is it on April 4th? Why is it that Mothers' Day and Fathers' Day are normal workdays, yet Children's Day is a national holiday?

I. Origin　

1. Purpose　

The purpose of a Children's Day is to remind people to protect the rights of children, such as living and receiving an education.

2. The First Ever Children's Day　

The first-ever Children's Day was observed in June 1867 when a pope held a special ceremony for local children on the second Sunday of every June. He called it "Rose Day," then "Flower Sunday," and finally "Children's Day."

However, the first country to declare a Children's Day is Turkey.

Although the people have been celebrating something similar back in 1920, it wasn't until 1929 when Turkish officials finally established a "National Sovereignty and Children's Day" on April 23rd.

3. Development of Children's Day　

In 1925, the World Conference on Child Welfare discussed the possibility of an "International Children's Day."

On November 4th, 1949, the Women's International Democratic Federation in Moscow, Russia, made June 1st the "International Day for Protection of Children."

After 1950, many communist and post-communist countries made June 1st their "Children's Day."

4. The United Nation　

On December 14th, 1954, India and Uruguay hope countries can have an "Universal Children's Day" during the conference.

On November 20th, 1959, the United Nations passed the Declaration of the Rights of the Child, officially making November 20th the "World Children's Day."

III. Chinas　

1. Republic of China (Taiwan)　

Minkuo Year (Common Era) Event
20 (1931) The Chinese Salesian Association proposed to make April 4th (4/4) the Children's Day, with the reason "It's Spring and has nice weather, plus 3/3, 5/5, 9/9, 10/10 are all public holidays." The Executive Yuan accepted it.
80 (1991) Combined with Women's Day, called "Women and Children's Holiday."
87 (1998) Elementary school students and below get a day off.
90 (2001) Became a national holiday, and everyone gets the day off.
91 (2002) If Children's Day coincided with the Ching Ming Festival (also known as Tomb Sweeping Day), then people get the day before (4/3) off. If it's on a Thursday then people get a day off on Friday.

2. People's Republic of China (Mainland China)　

On December 23rd, 1949, Chinese Communist Party passed a law which abolished the April 4th Children's Day set by Republic of China and copied the Soviet Union to use June 1st instead. They called it "June 1st International Children's Day" and elemantary school students get half a day off. After 1956, everyone got the day off.

Schools usually hosted talent shows or field trips the day before. People who worked for the government could receive gifts from the Chinese Communist Party if they had children under 14.

Children under 14 could enter the Forbidden City for free, while accompanying adults paid half the fee.

3. Hong Kong SAR　

Hong Kong celebrates Children's Day as the Republic of China does, but it is not a national holiday, and therefore people do not take a day off.

4. Macau SAR　

Macao, which has always been following the rules of PRC, celebrates the International Children's Day with the mainland.
